Hi, I'm Claire Finley , an astronomy PhD student at The University of Texas at Austin working with Prof. Brendan P. Bowler (UC Santa Barbara). My research focuses on how giant planets form and evolve, with an emphasis on directly imaged exoplanets and their circumplanetary disks.

I'm currently leading a multiwavelength photometric program to characterize the accretion and circumplanetary disk properties of directly-imaged exoplanets using the Hubble Space Telescope and James Webb Space Telescope. Previously, I studied the parent volatile species in comet 41P/Tuttle-Giacobini-Kresák as an intern at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center using data from NIRSPEC on Keck II, and performed research on ionized star formation in bright-rimmed clouds as an REU student at the Green Bank Observatory. I earned my Bachelor's of Science in Astronomy and Astrophysics from Villanova University in 2023.

If you're interested in my work or potential collaborations, I'd love to connect! Please feel free to reach out via email or check out my CV linked below.
